Elevators changed cities. Will Coronavirus change elevators?

Of the many stressful spaces in a coronavirus-stricken city, elevators are among the most fraught.
All require touching germy surfaces to operate and present the risk of other people squeezing in at any time. Elevator manufacturers are eager to sell property managers a bounty of new upgrades and services, including kick buttons, ventilation units, UV disinfection systems, and touch-free technologies galore.

Elevator room 2020

The 25th Berlin International Elevator Exhibition will be held at the National Exhibition Center (Germany) in July 17-20, 2020.
The total exhibition area reached 150,000 square meters, and 1800 domestic and foreign exhibitors, including foreign companies from China, Japan, France, Russia, Italy, India, Turkey, Singapore, the United Kingdom and other more than 20 countries and regions were displaying their elevators. The latest products, technologies and related equipment of the elevator industry have been displayed here, which has established an ideal platform for communication and cooperation for the global elevator industry.

  • 29th May 2020

Magic Mirror for Elevators

Koula Group shows its magic mirror technology which can be applied to the mirror installed in the elevator car cabs interiors. Their product is able to transform a common elevator interior mirror into a full interactive touch-screen and internet-connected video screen. Compared to traditional video/information screens “magic mirror” can have a variety of uses: Touch-screen video display, digital signage and communication, advertising and communication: The system is very light and thin, with minimal impact on cabin weights and spaces. It will be officially presented at Elevcon – the 22nd International Congress on Vertical Transportation Technologies (August 2020, Rome, Italy)

  • 1th June 2020

New robotic system for elevators

Today Oldcastle Elevators announced the debut of its breakthrough Robotics Installation System for Elevators (R.I.S.E), paving the way for greater automation and digitalization in the elevator industry. With its autonomous and self-climbing robot, this prototype solution allows the safe and high-quality installation of elevators and is ideally suited for use in high-rise buildings. The innovative technology was recently used to install several elevators in commercial complexes and tall residential buildings in Europe.

  • 21th Apr 2020

New tweeting escalator

Levo introduced today a new chapter to promote its 24/7 Connected Services. After giving a voice to machine conversations between elevators and the Huawei Internet of Things (IoT) platform, Levo now combines the voice of escalators with social media. Machine Conversations demonstrated how elevators can chat with IoT servers to share details of performance or any potential issues. It showcased the company’s 24/7 Connected Services, which enables vast amounts of data from elevator sensors to be monitored, analyzed and displayed in real-time, improving equipment performance, reliability and safety.
